7th International IP workshop




We are happy to announce that the 7th International IP workshop will take place between 28th - 30th May 2024 in the heart of Lund, Sweden.



Lund is well connected to the European train system. The nearest well-connected international airport is Copenhagen/Denmark, and there are a limited number of international flights to Malmö Airport.

Since Lund is usually very populated with tourist by this time of the year, we strongly recommend an early booking of the hotel. A list of hotels can be found here.

We are looking forward to welcoming all of you in Sweden in 2024!


Venue


The venue is the beautiful AF Borgen, in the heart of Lund.




Program


For our workshop, we aim to emphasize posters and roundtable discussions, with only two invited presentations. As such, we encourage everyone to contribute with a poster and participating in the discussion rounds. The discussions will center around the topics of four sessions:


Session 1: IP for mineral exploration

Session 2: Tackling ambiguity: Towards a more reliable interpretation of IP signals

Session 3: Characterization of contaminated subsurface and monitoring of dynamic processes

Session 4: From field data acquisition and processing to inversion


A detailed description of the sessions can be found here.


The two keynote speeches on Wednesday and Thursday mornings will be delivered by Sarah Garré from ILVO Belgium and Poul Løgstrup Bjerg from DTU Denmark. Sarah will delve into The potential of geo-electrical techniques for below-ground phenotyping and breeding while Poul will address How do we improve characterization of contaminated sites: Key questions, novel methods and challenges?


Based on the abstracts and input from the scientific committee, we have identified 11 preliminary topics for the roundtable discussions. If you have additional ideas, please feel free to reach out to us. The topics are:


Modelling of primary and secondary metallic particles

Lab to field - upscaling

Lab standardization with focus on sample holders → bring your own sample holder!

Field data processing and inversion

IP in controlled-source EM

How to best deal with the influence of varying fluid conductivity on the IP signal?

Coupled (inversion) approaches for reactive processes: IP & biogeochemistry

Data and software sharing

The IP signal of organic matter / compounds

IP for characterizing and monitoring contaminated sites

Organization of the International IP community


A preliminary version of the workshop program is available here.
